Algorithms Specialization — Tim Roughgarden

Course Description

This specialization provides a rigorous introduction to algorithms and their design, analysis, and implementation. It focuses on building a strong theoretical and practical understanding of how efficient algorithms are constructed and evaluated.

Students learn to solve complex computational problems using structured approaches such as divide and conquer, greedy algorithms, and dynamic programming. The course also emphasizes analyzing time and space complexity and understanding trade-offs in algorithm design.

Instructor Details

Tim Roughgarden is a professor at Stanford University and a leading expert in algorithms and computational theory. His teaching style emphasizes clarity, intuition, and deep understanding of fundamental principles.

Course Curriculum

  • Divide and Conquer Algorithms
  • Graph Algorithms (Shortest Paths, Minimum Spanning Trees)
  • Greedy Algorithms
  • Dynamic Programming
  • Complexity Analysis
  • NP-Completeness and Problem Reductions

The specialization combines mathematical foundations with real-world applications and problem-solving strategies.

Duration

6–8 weeks
